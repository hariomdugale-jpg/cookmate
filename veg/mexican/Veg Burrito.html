1. Cook the Rice & Beans
Cook rice as per packet instructions and set aside. In a separate pan, heat a little oil and warm the drained kidney beans with a pinch of cumin, red chilli powder and salt. Mash lightly with a fork to create a thick, chunky texture with some whole beans remaining. The beans should be well spiced and slightly dry — not watery. Keep warm.
2. Make the Spiced Veggie Filling
Heat olive oil in a pan over high heat. Add sliced onion and cook for 3–4 minutes until lightly charred at the edges. Add minced garlic and sliced capsicum and stir fry for 2–3 minutes until softened but still slightly crunchy. Season with red chilli powder, cumin powder and salt. Stir in 2 tablespoons of tomato salsa and cook for 1 more minute until everything is coated and fragrant. Squeeze in a little lemon juice and toss. Set aside.
3. Warm the Tortillas
Heat a dry pan or tawa over medium heat. Warm each tortilla for 20–30 seconds per side until soft, pliable and lightly toasted. Keep them wrapped in a clean cloth to stay warm while you assemble. Cold tortillas crack when folded — always warm them first.
4. Assemble the Burrito
Lay a warm tortilla flat on a clean surface. Spread a generous spoonful of sour cream down the centre. Layer a scoop of rice, a spoonful of spiced kidney beans and a portion of the veggie filling. Add shredded cheese on top so it melts slightly from the heat. Spoon over a little extra salsa and scatter fresh coriander. Do not overfill — leave at least 5 cm border on each side for folding.
5. Fold, Wrap & Serve
Fold the two sides of the tortilla inward over the filling. Fold the bottom edge up tightly over the filling and roll firmly forward to seal into a tight burrito. Place seam-side down. For a crispy finish, toast the wrapped burrito in a hot pan for 1–2 minutes per side until golden and sealed. Slice diagonally and serve immediately with extra salsa and sour cream on the side.
