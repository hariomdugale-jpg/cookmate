1. Make the Fresh Salsa
Finely dice fresh tomatoes, onion and green chilli or jalapeños. Mix together with lime juice, chopped coriander and salt. Stir well and let it rest for 10 minutes. This fresh pico de gallo salsa is the heart of great nachos — do not use bottled salsa if you can avoid it.
2. Make the Guacamole
Scoop ripe avocado flesh into a bowl. Mash with a fork keeping it slightly chunky. Mix in lime juice, finely diced onion, chopped coriander, green chilli and salt. Taste and adjust. Press plastic wrap directly on the surface to prevent browning until ready to use.
3. Prepare the Bean & Corn Topping
Heat a little oil in a pan. Add drained rajma or black beans and sweet corn. Season with chilli powder, paprika, cumin and salt. Toss on medium heat for 3–4 minutes until warmed through and slightly caramelised. This savory topping adds heartiness and protein to the nachos.
4. Preheat the Oven
Preheat your oven to 200°C (390°F). Line a large baking tray with foil or parchment paper. Spread the tortilla chips in a single even layer — overlapping slightly is fine but avoid piling them too thick so every chip gets cheese and toppings.
5. Load the Nachos
Scatter the bean and corn mixture evenly over the chips. Add sliced jalapeños or green chillies. Grate a generous and even layer of cheddar and mozzarella cheese all over the top — be very generous with the cheese, it is the star of the show. Every chip should have some cheese on it.
6. Bake Until Melted
Bake in the preheated oven for 8–10 minutes until the cheese is completely melted, bubbly and lightly golden in spots. Keep a close eye — the chips can go from perfect to burnt quickly. Remove as soon as the cheese is fully melted and gooey.
7. Top & Serve Immediately
Remove from oven and immediately spoon fresh salsa, dollops of guacamole and sour cream all over the hot cheesy nachos. Scatter fresh coriander and a squeeze of lime. Serve straight from the tray immediately — nachos go soggy quickly so eat them right away!
