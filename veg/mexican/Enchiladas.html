Veg Enchiladas

Restaurant Style Recipe

1. Make the Enchilada Sauce
Heat a little oil in a saucepan over medium heat. Add minced garlic and cook for 1 minute. Add tomato puree, red chilli powder, cumin powder and salt. Stir well and cook for 3–4 minutes until the sauce darkens and thickens slightly. Add a splash of water to loosen to a pourable consistency. Simmer on low for 5 minutes until rich and fragrant. This red enchilada sauce is the soul of the dish — taste and adjust seasoning generously.
2. Make the Veggie Filling
Heat olive oil in a pan over medium-high heat. Add sliced onion and cook for 3–4 minutes until softened. Add minced garlic and sliced capsicum and cook for 2–3 minutes. Add drained kidney beans, a pinch of cumin, red chilli powder and salt. Mash a few beans lightly and mix everything together. Stir in 2 tablespoons of tomato salsa and cook for 2 more minutes until the filling is thick, well-spiced and dry. Set aside to cool slightly.
3. Warm & Fill the Tortillas
Warm each tortilla in a dry pan for 20 seconds per side until just pliable — do not let them go crispy or they will crack when rolled. Dip each warm tortilla briefly into the enchilada sauce to lightly coat it on both sides — this is the authentic technique that gives enchiladas their deep flavour all the way through. Place 2–3 tablespoons of the veggie filling and a sprinkle of cheese down the centre of each tortilla. Roll tightly and place seam-side down in a greased baking dish.
4. Top & Bake
Pour the remaining enchilada sauce evenly over all the rolled tortillas in the baking dish — every inch should be covered. Spoon over extra salsa if desired. Cover generously with shredded mozzarella and cheddar cheese. Bake in a preheated oven at 190°C for 20–25 minutes until the cheese is fully melted, bubbling and golden at the edges. The sauce should be thick and glossy around the enchiladas.
5. Garnish & Serve
Remove from oven and let rest for 5 minutes. Top with dollops of cool sour cream, a generous scatter of fresh coriander and a spoonful of salsa. Serve hot directly from the baking dish. Each portion should have 2–3 enchiladas scooped out with plenty of the saucy cheese. Pairs perfectly with a side of rice or nachos.
