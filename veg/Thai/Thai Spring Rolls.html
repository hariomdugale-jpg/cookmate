Thai Spring Rolls

1. Make the Filling
Heat a little oil in a wok over high heat. Add minced garlic and stir fry for 30 seconds. Add julienned carrot, sliced capsicum, mushrooms and spring onion. Stir fry on high heat for 2–3 minutes until just softened but still with crunch. Add crumbled or diced tofu. Season with soy sauce, salt and black pepper. Toss well and cook for 1 more minute until the filling is dry — a wet filling will make the wrappers soggy and burst during frying. Spread on a plate and let it cool completely before wrapping.
2. Soften the Wrappers
If using dried spring roll sheets, keep them covered under a damp cloth to prevent cracking. If using fresh wrappers, they are ready to use straight away. Work with one wrapper at a time, keeping the rest covered. Do not let them dry out or they will tear when you try to fold them.
3. Roll the Spring Rolls
Lay a wrapper flat in a diamond shape. Place 2–3 tablespoons of cooled filling in the lower centre. Fold the bottom corner up over the filling. Fold both side corners in tightly. Roll forward firmly from bottom to top. Seal the top corner with a dab of water or a flour-water paste. The roll should be tight with no air pockets — loose rolls burst open in the oil. Repeat until all filling is used.
4. Fry Until Golden
Heat oil in a deep pan to 175–180°C. Test the oil by dipping the tip of a spring roll in — it should sizzle immediately. Carefully slide in 3–4 spring rolls at a time, seam-side down. Fry for 3–4 minutes, turning occasionally, until deep golden brown and very crispy all over. Do not overcrowd the pan or the temperature drops and the rolls absorb oil. Drain on paper towels.
5. Serve
Serve immediately while piping hot and shattering crispy. Arrange on a plate and serve with sweet chilli dipping sauce on the side. Spring rolls go soft quickly — eat them right away. Garnish with fresh coriander or spring onion greens for colour.
