Veg Tom Yum Soup

Restaurant Style Recipe

1. Prepare the Aromatics
Bruise lemongrass stalks with the back of a knife to release the oils. Slice galangal or ginger into thin rounds. Tear kaffir lime leaves in half. Slit green chillies. These aromatics are the signature of Tom Yum and cannot be skipped — they give the soup its unmistakable fragrance.
2. Make the Broth Base
Bring 4 cups of water or vegetable stock to a boil in a pot. Add lemongrass, galangal/ginger, kaffir lime leaves and green chillies. Simmer on medium heat for 8–10 minutes so the aromatics fully infuse their flavour and fragrance into the broth.
3. Add Vegetables & Tofu
Add sliced mushrooms, julienned carrot, tofu cubes and the white parts of spring onion to the broth. Cook on medium heat for 5–6 minutes until the vegetables are just tender but still have a slight bite to them.
4. Add Coconut Milk
Pour in coconut milk and stir gently. The coconut milk transforms the clear spicy broth into a rich, creamy and aromatic soup. Let it simmer on low heat for 3–4 minutes — do not boil vigorously after adding coconut milk as it can split.
5. Season the Soup
Add soy sauce, chilli paste and salt to taste. Stir gently. The soup should be spicy, tangy and slightly rich. Adjust the heat with more chilli paste if desired and the saltiness with soy sauce. Every bowl of Tom Yum should be bold and punchy.
6. Add Lime Juice
Remove from heat. Squeeze in fresh lime or lemon juice generously. The sourness from fresh lime is what defines Tom Yum — add it off the heat so the bright citrus flavour stays fresh and does not turn bitter from prolonged cooking.
7. Garnish & Serve
Ladle into bowls. Garnish with chopped spring onion greens, fresh coriander and an extra slice of lime. Serve immediately piping hot on its own or with steamed jasmine rice on the side.
