Veg Pad Thai

Restaurant Style Recipe

Steps (Click for details)

1. Soak & Prepare Noodles
Soak rice noodles in warm water for 20–30 minutes until soft and pliable but not fully cooked. Drain and toss with a little oil to prevent sticking. Do not boil — the noodles will finish cooking in the wok.
2. Make the Pad Thai Sauce
Mix together tamarind paste, soy sauce, sugar and a pinch of chilli flakes in a small bowl. Stir until the sugar dissolves completely. Taste and adjust — the sauce should be a perfect balance of tangy, sweet and salty. This sauce is the soul of Pad Thai.
3. Fry the Tofu
Press tofu to remove excess moisture. Cut into small cubes. Heat oil in a wok over high heat. Fry the tofu for 4–5 minutes until golden and crispy on all sides. Remove and set aside. Crispy tofu adds a wonderful texture contrast to the soft noodles.
4. Scramble the Eggs
Push the contents of the wok to one side. Add a little oil to the empty side and crack in the eggs. Scramble them quickly on high heat until just set but still soft. Mix into the rest of the wok ingredients. The egg adds richness and binds the dish together.
5. Stir-Fry Garlic & Vegetables
Add oil to the wok. Add minced garlic and the white parts of spring onion. Stir-fry for 1 minute on high flame. Add bean sprouts and toss for 1–2 minutes keeping them crunchy. High heat is essential for that classic smoky wok-fried flavour.
6. Add Noodles & Sauce
Add the soaked noodles to the wok. Pour the Pad Thai sauce over everything. Toss rapidly on high heat for 2–3 minutes until the noodles absorb the sauce and turn a beautiful golden colour. Add the fried tofu and scrambled eggs back in and toss together.
7. Garnish & Serve
Plate the Pad Thai and top with crushed roasted peanuts, fresh spring onion greens and chilli flakes. Serve with a wedge of lime on the side. Squeeze the lime over just before eating for that essential fresh tangy finish.
