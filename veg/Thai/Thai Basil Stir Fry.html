1. Prep Everything First
Stir frying moves fast — prep all ingredients before you turn on the heat. Press the tofu between paper towels for 10 minutes to remove excess moisture then cut into cubes. Slice the capsicum and mushrooms. Mince the garlic and green chilli. Separate the Thai basil leaves. Have all sauces measured and ready to pour. Once the wok is hot there is no time to chop.
2. Crisp the Tofu
Heat oil in a wok or wide pan over very high heat. Add tofu cubes in a single layer and fry without stirring for 2–3 minutes until golden and crispy on one side. Flip and fry another 2 minutes. Remove and set aside. Crispy tofu holds its shape in the stir fry instead of turning mushy.
3. Stir Fry the Aromatics
In the same wok on maximum heat, add a little more oil. Add minced garlic and green chilli and stir fry for 30 seconds until fragrant and slightly golden — work fast, garlic burns quickly on high heat. Add sliced onion and toss for 1 minute until slightly softened.
4. Add Vegetables
Add the sliced capsicum and mushrooms to the wok. Toss and stir fry on high heat for 2–3 minutes. The vegetables should stay vibrant and have a slight crunch — do not overcook. The wok should be smoking hot the entire time for that authentic charred wok flavour.
5. Sauce & Finish
Return the crispy tofu to the wok. Pour in soy sauce, oyster sauce and a pinch of sugar. Toss everything together rapidly for 1–2 minutes until the sauce coats every piece and caramelises slightly. Turn off the heat and immediately fold in the fresh Thai basil leaves — they will wilt beautifully from the residual heat.
6. Serve
Transfer to a plate immediately. Garnish with sliced spring onion and extra fresh basil. Serve hot over steamed jasmine rice. Stir fry must be eaten the moment it is done — it loses its texture and heat quickly once plated.
