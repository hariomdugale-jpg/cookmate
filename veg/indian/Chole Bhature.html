Chole Bhature

1. Soak & Boil the Chickpeas
Wash chickpeas and soak in plenty of water overnight or for at least 8 hours. Drain and pressure cook with water, salt and a tea bag (for dark colour) for 4–5 whistles until soft and fully cooked. The tea bag gives the chole its signature dark rich colour.
2. Prepare the Bhature Dough
In a bowl mix maida, curd, a pinch of baking soda, salt and a little oil. Knead into a soft smooth dough adding water as needed. Cover with a damp cloth and rest for at least 2 hours at room temperature. The curd and resting time makes the bhature soft and puffy.
3. Cook the Chole Masala
Heat oil in a pan. Add finely chopped onions and fry until deep golden brown. Add ginger-garlic paste and cook for 2 minutes. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until mushy and oil separates. Add red chilli powder, turmeric, chole masala and garam masala. Cook the masala well for 4–5 minutes.
4. Add Chickpeas & Simmer
Add the boiled chickpeas along with some of the cooking water to the masala. Mash a few chickpeas against the side of the pan to thicken the gravy naturally. Add more chole masala if needed. Simmer on low heat for 15–20 minutes until the gravy is thick, dark and deeply flavourful.
5. Roll the Bhature
Divide the rested dough into equal portions. Roll each portion into an oval or round shape about 6–7 inches wide and medium thickness — not too thin or they won't puff up. Keep the rolled bhature covered while you fry them one at a time.
6. Fry the Bhature
Heat oil in a deep pan until very hot. Slide in a bhature and gently press with a slotted spoon so it puffs up. Fry for 1–2 minutes until golden on one side, flip and fry the other side. Drain on paper towels. Fry each bhature fresh and serve immediately — they deflate quickly.
7. Garnish & Serve
Garnish the chole with fresh coriander, sliced ginger julienne and a squeeze of lemon. Serve the hot puffed bhature alongside the chole with sliced onion, green chutney and pickle on the side.
