1. Soak the Dal & Rajma
Wash the whole black urad dal and rajma thoroughly. Soak both together in plenty of water for 8–10 hours or overnight — they must be soaked or they will never cook through properly. Drain before cooking. The urad dal should have swelled to nearly double in size and the rajma should be visibly plumper and softened.
2. Pressure Cook the Dal
Add the soaked dal and rajma to a pressure cooker with enough fresh water to cover by 5 cm. Add salt and a pinch of turmeric. Pressure cook on medium heat for 8–10 whistles until both are completely soft, tender and the urad dal is almost melting. Open the lid and roughly mash about one quarter of the dal with a ladle against the sides — this creates a thick, creamy base while keeping some whole grains for texture.
3. Build the Makhani Masala
Heat butter and ghee together in a heavy-bottomed pan over medium heat. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter. Add finely chopped onion and cook for 8–10 minutes until deeply golden and caramelised — do not rush this step. Add garlic and ginger and cook for 2 minutes. Add red chilli powder, turmeric and garam masala and stir for 30 seconds. Add tomato puree and cook on medium heat for 8–10 minutes until the butter separates from the masala and the mixture turns a deep red colour.
4. Slow Simmer the Dal
Pour the cooked dal and rajma into the masala and mix well. Add a little water to adjust consistency — Dal Makhani should be thick but pourable, not stiff. Bring to a gentle boil then reduce to the lowest heat possible. Cover partially and simmer for at least 45–60 minutes, stirring every 10 minutes to prevent sticking. The longer it simmers the richer and creamier it becomes. Restaurant-style Dal Makhani is sometimes simmered for 4–6 hours on a very low flame.
5. Finish with Cream & Serve
Stir in fresh cream and a generous knob of cold butter. Simmer for 5 more minutes until the cream is fully incorporated and the dal looks glossy, rich and deeply coloured. Taste and adjust salt and spice. Serve hot in a bowl topped with a swirl of cream, a small cube of butter and fresh coriander. Best enjoyed with naan, paratha or steamed basmati rice.
