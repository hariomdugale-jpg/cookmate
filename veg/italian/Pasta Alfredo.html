1. Cook the Pasta
Bring a large pot of water to a rolling boil. Add a generous amount of salt — the water should taste like the sea. Cook the fettuccine or pasta according to packet instructions until al dente — firm to the bite, not soft. Before draining, reserve at least 1 cup of the starchy pasta water. This cloudy water is liquid gold — it will help bring your sauce together silkily. Drain and toss with a tiny drizzle of olive oil to prevent sticking.
2. Make the Alfredo Sauce
In a wide pan over medium-low heat, melt the butter. Add minced garlic and cook gently for 1–2 minutes until fragrant but not browned — burnt garlic will ruin the delicate flavour of Alfredo. Pour in the fresh cream and stir to combine. Let it warm through on low heat for 2–3 minutes — do not boil. The sauce should be pale, silky and gently steaming.
3. Add the Parmesan
Remove the pan from heat. Add the grated Parmesan cheese in two or three batches, stirring constantly between each addition until fully melted and smooth. The residual heat is enough to melt the cheese without splitting the sauce. Season with salt and a generous crack of black pepper. The sauce should be thick, glossy and coat the back of a spoon beautifully.
4. Toss the Pasta
Add the drained hot pasta directly into the sauce and toss well using tongs or two forks. Add the reserved pasta water a little at a time — a splash or two — tossing as you go to loosen the sauce and help it cling to every strand. The starch in the pasta water emulsifies the sauce making it luxuriously creamy. The pasta should be glossy and well coated, not dry or clumped.
5. Plate & Serve
Twirl the pasta into warm bowls using tongs. Finish with an extra grating of Parmesan, a heavy crack of black pepper and chopped fresh parsley. Add a final knob of cold butter on top if desired for extra richness. Serve immediately — Alfredo sauce thickens and dries out quickly once off the heat, so it must always be eaten the moment it is ready.
