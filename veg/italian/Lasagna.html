1. Make the Meat Sauce (Bolognese)
Heat olive oil in a wide pan over medium-high heat. Add finely chopped onion and cook for 5 minutes until soft and golden. Add minced garlic and cook for 1 minute. Add the mince and cook for 8–10 minutes, breaking it apart constantly, until fully browned with no pink remaining. Pour in tomato puree and stir well. Add oregano, dried basil, salt and black pepper. Reduce heat and simmer for 20–25 minutes until the sauce is thick, rich and deep red. The longer it simmers the better it tastes.
2. Make the Béchamel (White Sauce)
Melt butter in a saucepan over medium heat. Add 2 tablespoons of flour and stir constantly for 1–2 minutes to cook out the raw flour taste — this is the roux. Gradually pour in the fresh cream, whisking constantly to prevent lumps. Keep whisking on medium heat for 4–5 minutes until the sauce thickens to a smooth, pourable consistency. Season with salt, black pepper and a pinch of nutmeg if available. The béchamel should coat a spoon thickly.
3. Cook the Lasagna Sheets
Bring a large pot of salted water to boil. Cook the lasagna sheets for 2–3 minutes less than packet instructions — they will continue cooking in the oven. Drain and lay flat on an oiled tray, not overlapping, to prevent sticking. If using oven-ready sheets, skip this step entirely — they go in dry and cook in the oven moisture.
4. Layer the Lasagna
Preheat oven to 190°C. In a deep baking dish, spread a thin layer of meat sauce on the base. Lay lasagna sheets over it in a single layer. Spread a layer of meat sauce, then a layer of béchamel, then a sprinkle of mozzarella. Repeat the layers — sheets, meat, béchamel, cheese — until all ingredients are used, finishing with a generous top layer of béchamel. Cover the entire top with grated mozzarella and Parmesan.
5. Bake & Serve
Cover the baking dish tightly with foil and bake at 190°C for 30 minutes. Remove the foil and bake uncovered for a further 15–20 minutes until the top is golden, bubbling and slightly caramelised at the edges. Remove from oven and rest for 10–15 minutes before cutting — this is essential, resting allows the layers to set so it holds its shape when sliced. Garnish with fresh parsley and serve with extra Parmesan.
