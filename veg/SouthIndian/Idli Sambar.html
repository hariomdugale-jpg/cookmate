Idli Sambar

1. Soak & Grind the Idli Batter
Wash and soak idli rice and urad dal separately in water for 6–8 hours or overnight. Drain and grind the urad dal first to a very smooth, fluffy batter — adding a little cold water while grinding. Then grind the rice to a slightly coarse batter. Combine both, add salt and mix well with your hand (body warmth helps fermentation). Cover and ferment at room temperature for 8–12 hours until the batter doubles in volume and smells pleasantly sour. Well-fermented batter is the secret to soft, spongy idlis.
2. Steam the Idlis
Grease the idli moulds generously with oil. Gently stir the fermented batter without deflating it — do not over-mix. Fill each mould about three quarters full. Place in a steamer with boiling water and steam on medium heat for 10–12 minutes. Do not open the lid during steaming. Test with a toothpick — it should come out clean. Let them rest for 2 minutes before unmoulding. Wet a spoon with water and slide it around the edges to release each idli cleanly.
3. Cook the Toor Dal
Wash the toor dal and pressure cook with turmeric, salt and enough water for 3–4 whistles until completely soft and mushy. Open the lid and whisk the dal smooth. Meanwhile soak tamarind in 100 ml warm water for 10 minutes and squeeze out the pulp. Set both aside — these form the two base components of the sambar.
4. Make the Sambar
Heat oil in a pot. Add mustard seeds and let them splutter. Add dry red chilli and curry leaves. Add sliced onion and cook for 3–4 minutes until golden. Add chopped tomato and cook until mushy. Stir in sambar powder and cook for 1 minute. Add the tamarind water and bring to a boil. Pour in the cooked toor dal, add water to adjust consistency and simmer for 10–12 minutes until the sambar is deeply flavoured, slightly tangy and fragrant. Adjust salt.
5. Temper & Serve
For the final tempering, heat a small pan with oil. Add mustard seeds, curry leaves and a dry red chilli. Once they splutter, pour this directly into the sambar — the sizzle releases the full aroma. Serve the soft idlis hot in a bowl with a generous ladle of sambar poured over them and coconut chutney on the side. Idli sambar is best eaten immediately while both are piping hot.
