1. Soak & Grind the Batter
Wash and soak dosa rice and urad dal separately in plenty of water for 6–8 hours or overnight. Drain and grind each separately in a wet grinder or mixer to a smooth, slightly coarse batter. Combine both, add salt and mix well. The batter should be pourable like thick pancake batter. Cover and ferment at room temperature for 8–12 hours until the batter rises and turns slightly sour and bubbly. Fermentation is the soul of a great dosa — do not skip it.
2. Make the Potato Masala
Boil potatoes until fully tender, then peel and roughly mash — leave some chunky pieces for texture. Heat oil in a pan. Add mustard seeds and let them splutter. Add curry leaves, sliced onion and slit green chillies and cook for 4–5 minutes until the onion softens. Add turmeric powder and stir for 30 seconds. Add the mashed potato and mix well. Season with salt and fold in fresh coriander. The masala should be dry, well-spiced and golden yellow. Set aside.
3. Heat & Season the Tawa
Heat a flat iron tawa or non-stick pan over medium-high heat. The tawa must be very hot before pouring batter. Sprinkle a few drops of water on the surface — if they sizzle and evaporate instantly, the tawa is ready. Wipe the surface with a cut onion dipped in oil — this seasons the tawa and prevents sticking. This step is key for a crispy dosa.
4. Pour & Spread the Dosa
Pour one ladle of fermented batter in the centre of the hot tawa. Immediately using the back of the ladle, spread the batter in fast circular outward motions into a thin, even round. The thinner you spread it the crispier it gets. Drizzle a little oil or ghee around the edges and on top. Cook on medium-high heat for 2–3 minutes until the edges turn golden and the surface looks set and dry.
5. Fill, Fold & Serve
Place a generous spoonful of potato masala in the centre of the dosa while it is still on the tawa. Fold the dosa over the filling from both sides to form a cone or roll shape. Slide off the tawa immediately onto a plate. Serve piping hot with coconut chutney and sambar on the side. Masala dosa must always be eaten fresh — it loses its crispiness within minutes.
