1. Dry Roast the Moong Dal
Heat a heavy-bottomed pot or pressure cooker on medium flame without any oil. Add the yellow moong dal and dry roast it, stirring continuously, for 3–4 minutes until it turns lightly golden and gives off a nutty, earthy aroma. Do not let it brown too much. This roasting step deepens the flavour of the dal and is the secret to a truly fragrant Pongal. Remove and set aside.
2. Cook Rice and Dal Together
Rinse the raw rice and the roasted moong dal together under cold water. Add both to a pressure cooker with 4–5 cups of water and a pinch of salt. Pressure cook on medium heat for 4–5 whistles until both the rice and dal are completely soft and mushy — almost porridge-like. This is the texture you want. Open after the pressure drops naturally. Mash gently with a ladle. If using a pot, cook covered with extra water for 25–30 minutes until fully soft.
3. Prepare the Tempering (Tadka)
Heat a generous amount of ghee in a separate small pan — do not be stingy here, ghee is what makes Pongal luxurious. Add cashew nuts and fry until golden. Remove the cashews and set aside. In the same ghee, add whole black peppercorns and cumin seeds; let them sizzle for 20 seconds. Add finely chopped or grated fresh ginger, slit green chillies and curry leaves — they will splutter, so be careful. Finish with a pinch of asafoetida and stir for 30 seconds.
4. Combine & Season
Pour the sizzling tempering along with the fried cashews directly into the cooked rice-dal mixture. Mix well. Taste and adjust salt. Add a little more hot water if the Pongal has thickened too much — it should flow slowly like a thick porridge, not be stiff. The final texture should be soft, creamy and flowing. Finish with an extra drizzle of ghee on top for richness and aroma.
5. Serve Hot
Serve Pongal piping hot in a bowl or on a banana leaf for the most authentic experience. Accompany with coconut chutney and a bowl of hot sambar. A side of crispy vadai is the classic pairing. Pongal is best eaten fresh and hot — it thickens as it cools, though you can loosen it again with a little warm water. This dish is simplicity perfected: do not rush it, and do not hold back on the ghee.
