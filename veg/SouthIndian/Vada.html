Vada

Crispy South Indian Lentil Doughnut

About Dish

Steps (Click for details)

1. Soak the Urad Dal
Wash the whole skinned urad dal thoroughly under cold water. Soak it in plenty of water for 4–5 hours — no longer, or the batter becomes too wet and will not hold its shape when frying. The dal should be plump and soft when you press it between your fingers. Drain completely before grinding. Removing as much water as possible at this stage is critical — a dry, fluffy batter is the secret to a perfectly crispy vada with a light interior.
2. Grind to a Thick Fluffy Batter
Grind the drained urad dal in a wet grinder or mixer with the least amount of water possible — add water only a teaspoon at a time if the grinder struggles. Grind until the batter is very smooth, white and incredibly fluffy. The batter should be thick enough to hold its shape when dropped from a spoon — it must not flow. To test: drop a small ball of batter into a bowl of water — it should float. If it sinks, the batter is too wet; you cannot fix wet batter, so grind carefully.
3. Mix in the Flavourings
Transfer the ground batter to a large bowl. Add finely chopped onion, green chilli, grated ginger, chopped curry leaves, fresh coriander, whole or coarsely crushed black peppercorns, cumin seeds, a pinch of asafoetida and salt. Mix well with your hand — using your hand incorporates air into the batter and keeps it light. Do not overmix. Taste and adjust seasoning. The batter should be savoury, aromatic and thick. Chill briefly if the batter feels too soft from the warmth of grinding.
4. Shape the Vadas
Wet your hands with water — this prevents sticking. Take a lemon-sized ball of batter and place it on a wet palm or a wet plastic sheet. Flatten it gently into a round disc about 1 cm thick. Using your thumb, poke a hole in the centre and gently widen it into a neat ring — like a doughnut. The hole helps the vada cook evenly through the centre. If shaping is difficult, you can skip the hole and make plain patties (these are called "paddu style"), though the ring shape is traditional and fries more evenly.
5. Deep Fry to Golden Perfection
Heat oil in a deep heavy pan to 175–180°C. The oil must be hot enough — test with a small drop of batter; it should rise to the surface within 2 seconds. Gently slide shaped vadas into the oil, 2–3 at a time. Do not overcrowd. Fry on medium heat for 3–4 minutes, turning once, until deep golden brown and crispy all over. Remove and drain on paper towels. The vada should be hollow and airy inside with a shatteringly crisp exterior. Serve immediately — piping hot — with coconut chutney and sambar.
