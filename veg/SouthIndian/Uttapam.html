Uttapam

Thick South Indian Savoury Pancake

About Dish

Steps (Click for details)

1. Prepare & Ferment the Batter
Wash and soak idli/dosa rice and urad dal separately for 6–8 hours or overnight. Drain and grind each to a smooth batter — the urad dal should be light and fluffy, the rice slightly coarse. Combine both, add salt and mix well. The batter should be thicker than a dosa batter — it needs to hold toppings without spreading too thin. Ferment at room temperature for 8–12 hours until bubbly and slightly sour. A well-fermented batter is the foundation of a great uttapam.
2. Prepare the Toppings
Finely chop the onion, tomato, green chilli, capsicum (if using), curry leaves and fresh coriander. Grate the ginger. Mix all the toppings together in a bowl with a pinch of crushed black pepper. Keep them ready before you start cooking — uttapam moves quickly on the tawa and you need to scatter toppings the moment batter is poured. The toppings should be finely cut so they press into the batter easily and cook through.
3. Heat & Season the Tawa
Heat a flat iron tawa or non-stick pan over medium heat. Unlike dosa, uttapam is cooked on medium — not high — heat, to allow the thick batter to cook through without burning. Sprinkle a few drops of water; if they dance and evaporate, the tawa is ready. Wipe the surface lightly with a cut onion dipped in oil to season it. A well-seasoned tawa prevents sticking and gives a beautiful base to the uttapam.
4. Pour, Top & Cook
Pour one large ladle of batter onto the tawa and spread gently into a thick round — about 6–7 inches wide and 1 cm thick. Do not spread too thin; the thickness is what defines uttapam. Immediately scatter a generous handful of the prepared toppings all over the surface. Press lightly with the back of the ladle so the toppings embed into the batter. Drizzle oil around the edges and a little on top. Cook on medium heat for 3–4 minutes until the base is golden and the edges look set.
5. Flip, Finish & Serve
Once the base is crispy and golden, carefully flip the uttapam using a flat spatula. The topping side will now be face-down on the tawa — cook for another 2 minutes until the vegetables are slightly charred and fragrant. The onion and tomato caramelise beautifully on contact with the hot tawa. Slide onto a plate, topping-side up. Serve immediately with coconut chutney and sambar. Uttapam is best eaten hot and fresh — do not let it sit.
