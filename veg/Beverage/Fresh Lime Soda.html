1. Make the Sugar Syrup
Combine equal parts sugar and water in a small saucepan. Heat on medium flame stirring until the sugar completely dissolves — about 2–3 minutes. Do not boil. Remove from heat and let it cool completely. This simple syrup blends smoothly into cold drinks unlike granulated sugar which sinks to the bottom.
2. Squeeze the Limes
Cut fresh limes in half and squeeze out the juice using a citrus juicer or by hand. Strain to remove seeds and pulp. For 1 glass you need approximately 2–3 tablespoons of fresh lime juice. Always use fresh limes — bottled juice cannot match the bright fresh flavour.
3. Mix the Base
In a tall glass combine the fresh lime juice with sugar syrup to taste. Add a generous pinch of black salt — this is what makes Indian style lime soda so unique and refreshing compared to plain lemonade. Add a tiny pinch of roasted cumin powder and black pepper for depth.
4. Choose Sweet or Salted
Decide your style — Sweet (meetha) or Salted (namkeen) or Mixed (both). For sweet add more sugar syrup. For salted skip extra sugar and add more black salt and a pinch of chaat masala. For mixed balance both. All three are equally popular across India and taste completely different.
5. Add Ice
Fill the glass generously with ice cubes. The ice is essential — Fresh Lime Soda must be served very cold. Crushed ice chills faster but regular cubes work perfectly. The cold temperature enhances the refreshing citrus flavour and the fizz of the soda.
6. Top with Club Soda
Pour chilled club soda or sparkling water slowly over the ice right up to the rim of the glass. Pour gently along the side to preserve the maximum fizz. Do not stir vigorously after adding soda — just give it one gentle swirl so the base mixes without losing the bubbles.
7. Garnish & Serve
Garnish with a sprig of fresh mint leaves and a lime wheel on the rim of the glass. Serve immediately while the soda is still fizzy and cold. Drink it fast — Fresh Lime Soda is at its absolute best the moment it is made.
