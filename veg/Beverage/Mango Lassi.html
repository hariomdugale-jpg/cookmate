1. Prepare the Mango
Peel the ripe mangoes and slice the flesh away from the seed. Cut into rough chunks and add to a blender. Alphonso or Kesar mangoes are ideal — their intense sweetness and deep golden colour make the best lassi. If mangoes are not in season, use good quality canned mango pulp as a substitute. Chilling the mango pieces for 30 minutes before blending gives a naturally cold and refreshing lassi.
2. Blend Everything Together
Add the yoghurt, chilled milk, sugar and a pinch of cardamom powder to the blender with the mango. Blend on high speed for 60–90 seconds until completely smooth, thick and frothy. Taste the lassi — adjust sugar if it needs more sweetness and cardamom for more fragrance. Add more milk if you prefer a thinner consistency or less milk for a thicker, creamier lassi. Blend once more for 20 seconds.
3. Chill & Serve
Pour the blended lassi into tall chilled glasses over ice cubes. The lassi should be a gorgeous deep golden yellow with a frothy top. Garnish with a sprinkle of crushed pistachios, a pinch of cardamom powder and a few strands of saffron soaked in a teaspoon of warm milk if available. Serve immediately ice cold — Mango Lassi is always best the moment it is made, before the froth settles.
