1. Boil the Noodles
Bring a large pot of water to a boil. Add salt and the hakka noodles. Cook for 1–2 minutes less than packet instructions — they should be just undercooked and still firm. They will finish cooking in the wok. Drain immediately and rinse under cold water to stop cooking and prevent sticking. Toss with a little oil to keep them separated. Spread on a plate and set aside.
2. Prep All Vegetables
Julienne the carrot, thinly slice the capsicum, shred the cabbage, slice the green chilli and chop the garlic finely. Separate the white and green parts of spring onion — whites go in while cooking, greens for garnish. Have everything ready before the wok goes on — hakka noodles cook in under 5 minutes total and there is no time to prep once you start.
3. Stir Fry the Vegetables
Heat oil in a wok over maximum heat until smoking. Add garlic and green chilli and toss for 20 seconds. Add the white spring onion parts and stir fry for 30 seconds. Add carrot and cabbage and toss on high heat for 2 minutes — they should stay crunchy. Add capsicum last and toss for 1 more minute. The wok must stay blazing hot the entire time for that smoky restaurant flavour.
4. Add Noodles & Sauces
Add the boiled noodles to the wok and toss everything together rapidly on high heat. Pour in soy sauce, vinegar, salt and black pepper. Toss and stir fry for 2–3 minutes until every strand of noodle picks up the sauce and turns a light golden brown. If the noodles stick, add a splash of water. The noodles should be slightly charred in places — that is the signature street-style flavour.
5. Serve
Transfer to a plate immediately. Garnish generously with the green parts of spring onion and fresh coriander. Serve piping hot on its own or with Manchurian or fried rice on the side. Hakka noodles must be eaten immediately — they clump and dry out as they cool.
