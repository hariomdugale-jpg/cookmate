Pizza Margherita

1. Make the Pizza Dough
Mix dry yeast and a pinch of sugar in warm water and let it sit for 5–10 minutes until frothy. In a large bowl combine maida and salt. Add the yeast mixture and olive oil. Knead for 8–10 minutes until the dough is smooth, soft and elastic. Shape into a ball, coat with olive oil, cover and let it rise for 1–1.5 hours until doubled in size.
2. Make the Tomato Sauce
Heat olive oil in a pan over medium heat. Add minced garlic and cook for 1 minute until fragrant. Add tomato puree, a pinch of sugar, salt and chilli flakes. Simmer on low heat for 10–12 minutes until the sauce thickens slightly and turns a deep red colour. Let it cool before spreading on the dough.
3. Preheat the Oven
Preheat your oven to the highest temperature possible — ideally 250°C (480°F) — for at least 30 minutes. If you have a pizza stone or baking steel, place it in the oven to preheat too. A very hot oven is essential for a crispy base and beautifully bubbly cheese.
4. Shape the Dough
Punch down the risen dough and divide into portions. On a lightly floured surface, stretch or roll the dough into a thin round about 25–30 cm wide. For authentic Neapolitan pizza, use your hands to stretch — the edges should be slightly thicker than the centre to form a natural crust.
5. Assemble the Pizza
Place the shaped dough on a greased baking tray or a floured pizza peel. Spread the tomato sauce evenly over the base leaving a 2 cm border for the crust. Tear fresh mozzarella into pieces and distribute evenly over the sauce. Drizzle lightly with olive oil and add a pinch of salt and chilli flakes.
6. Bake the Pizza
Bake in the preheated oven at 250°C for 8–12 minutes until the crust is golden and charred in spots and the cheese is melted, bubbly and lightly browned. Keep a close eye — at high temperatures the pizza can go from perfect to burnt very quickly.
7. Garnish & Serve
Remove from the oven and immediately scatter fresh basil leaves over the hot pizza. Drizzle with a little extra virgin olive oil. Slice and serve immediately — Pizza Margherita is best enjoyed the moment it comes out of the oven.
