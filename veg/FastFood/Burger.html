Classic Beef Burger

Restaurant Style Recipe

1. Make the Beef Patty
Place the beef mince in a bowl. Add minced garlic, salt and a very generous amount of coarsely crushed black pepper. Mix gently with your hands — do not overwork the meat or the patty will turn tough and dense. Divide into equal portions and shape each into a round patty slightly wider than the bun — they shrink as they cook. Press a small dimple in the centre of each patty with your thumb. This prevents the patty from puffing up into a dome while cooking. Refrigerate for 15 minutes to firm up.
2. Cook the Patty
Heat a cast iron pan or heavy skillet over very high heat until smoking hot. Add a little oil. Place the patties dimple-side up in the pan and press down firmly with a spatula. Cook on high heat for 3–4 minutes without moving until a deep brown crust forms on the bottom. Flip once and cook for another 2–3 minutes. In the last minute, place a slice of cheddar cheese on top and cover the pan with a lid for 1 minute to melt it perfectly. Remove and rest for 2 minutes.
3. Toast the Buns & Caramelise Onions
Slice the burger buns in half. Spread butter on the cut sides and toast face-down on the same hot pan for 1–2 minutes until golden and slightly crispy. In the same pan, add sliced onion rings and cook on medium heat for 6–8 minutes, stirring occasionally, until golden, soft and caramelised. Season with a pinch of salt. These sweet caramelised onions are a game-changer inside a burger.
4. Make the Burger Sauce
Mix mayonnaise with a spoonful of tomato ketchup and a pinch of black pepper in a small bowl. Stir until combined into a smooth, tangy burger sauce. This simple two-ingredient sauce is all you need — spread it generously on both the top and bottom bun. It adds creaminess and balances the richness of the beef patty beautifully.
5. Assemble & Serve
Spread burger sauce on the bottom toasted bun. Layer a few fresh lettuce leaves, then place the cheesy beef patty on top. Add the caramelised onions. Add a drizzle of extra ketchup if desired. Place the top bun on and press down firmly. Secure with a toothpick or skewer. Serve immediately while the patty is still hot and the cheese is melted. Serve with fries or nachos on the side.
